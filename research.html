Research

The social life of scientific knowledge

My work asks how science is understood, taught, and assessed as a social practice — and how that understanding shapes who gets to participate in it.

Doctoral research

The Nature of Science among Indian Students

My PhD thesis — Exploring the Knowledge Base of the Nature of Science among Indian Students: A Sociological Study in Bihar — investigates what students actually understand about how science works: its methods, the status of its claims, and its limits. Drawing on the sociology of education and STS, it treats the "nature of science" not as a fixed curriculum item but as a socially shaped form of knowledge, unevenly distributed across a student population.

Area I

Sociology & Philosophy of Science Education

How is science taught, and on what epistemological assumptions? This strand brings the sociology and philosophy of education to bear on science pedagogy — examining teaching epistemology, the nature of science, and the social conditions under which scientific knowledge is transmitted in Indian schools and colleges.

Area II

Public Understanding of Science & Scientific Literacy

Moving beyond the "deficit model," this work rethinks how scientific literacy is conceptualised and assessed. My conference papers propose new approaches to measuring what a public knows about science, and introduce the idea of "science space" as an alternative to the dominant notion of science capital.

Area III

STS, Epistemic Learning & Cognition

At the intersection of Science, Technology & Society and the study of learning, this strand examines epistemic cognition — how learners come to reason about knowledge and justification — and what an STS-informed mode of science education might look like.

Word in the margin Epistemic — relating to knowledge or to the conditions under which it is acquired and justified. Epistemic cognition is the thinking we do about knowledge itself: what we can know, and how we can be sure.
