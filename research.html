Research

The social life of scientific knowledge

My work asks how science is understood, taught, and assessed as a social practice — and how that understanding shapes who gets to participate in it.

Doctoral research

The Nature of Science among Indian Students

My PhD thesis — Exploring the Knowledge Base of the Nature of Science among Indian Students: A Sociological Study in Bihar — investigates what students actually understand about how science works: its methods, the status of its claims, and its limits. Drawing on the sociology of science education and STS, it treats the "nature of science" not as a fixed curriculum item but as a socially shaped form of knowledge, unevenly distributed across a student population.

Research area

Sociology of Science Education & Philosophy of Education

A social-science approach to science: how science is taught and on what epistemological assumptions. This strand brings the sociology and philosophy of education to bear on science pedagogy — examining teaching epistemology, the philosophy of science, and the nature of science.

Research area

Public Understanding of Science

How does a public come to understand science, and how is that understanding assessed? My conference papers propose new approaches to the assessment of scientific literacy, and introduce the idea of "science space" as an alternative to the dominant notion of science capital.

Research area

STS, Epistemic Learning & Cognition

At the intersection of Science, Technology & Society and the study of learning, this strand examines epistemic learning and cognition — how learners reason about knowledge and justification — and what an STS-informed mode of science education might look like.

Wider interests

Indian Society, Equity & Social Theory

Beyond science education, my interests extend to Indian society — its change and continuity, the socio-political issues affecting it with special reference to equity in education, and the relevance of constitutional morality — together with social theory and method.

Word in the margin Epistemic — relating to knowledge or to the conditions under which it is acquired and justified. Epistemic cognition is the thinking we do about knowledge itself: what we can know, and how we can be sure.
