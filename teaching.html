Teaching

In the classroom

I teach sociology as a craft of disciplined seeing — equal parts theory, method, and the patience to let evidence complicate a good story.

Philosophy

Theory you can carry into the field

A theory is worth teaching only if a student can take it outside the seminar room and use it to see something they would otherwise have missed. My courses pair canonical texts with raw fieldwork — a Weber reading beside a land record, a Durkheim chapter beside a marriage register — so that abstraction never floats free of the world it claims to explain.
