About

A sociologist of science, education, and the public

Ramjit Kumar is a PhD scholar in Sociology at IIT Patna and a UGC Junior Research Fellow, working at the meeting point of the sociology of education, the philosophy of science, and Science, Technology & Society.

My research begins from a simple but stubborn question: when a student learns science, what exactly are they learning? Not merely a set of facts, but an image of what science is — how it makes claims, where its authority comes from, and what it can and cannot do. My doctoral thesis, Exploring the Knowledge Base of the Nature of Science among Indian Students, pursues this question through a sociological study based in Bihar.

I am trained in both the interpretive and the quantitative traditions. I work comfortably with survey methods — scale development, factor analysis, structural equation modelling — and with mixed-method and qualitative tools. That methodological breadth reflects a conviction that the social study of science education needs both the precision of measurement and the depth of interpretation.

Beyond the thesis, my interests range across Indian society and its transformations, the relevance of constitutional morality, equity in education, and social theory and method — the wider intellectual context in which questions about science and knowledge come alive.
