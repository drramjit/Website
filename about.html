About

A sociologist of science, education, and the public

Dr. Ramjit Kumar is a PhD Candidate (JRF) in Sociology at the Department of Humanities & Social Sciences, IIT Patna, working at the meeting point of the sociology of science education, the philosophy of education, and Science, Technology & Society.

My research begins from a simple but stubborn question: when a student learns science, what exactly are they learning? Not merely a set of facts, but an image of what science is — how it makes claims, where its authority comes from, and what it can and cannot do. My doctoral thesis, Exploring the Knowledge Base of the Nature of Science among Indian Students: A Sociological Study in Bihar, pursues this question through the lens of the sociology of science education.

I take a social-science approach to science, with a sustained interest in teaching epistemology, the philosophy of science, the public understanding of science, and the nature of science. Alongside this runs a concern with epistemic learning and cognition — how learners come to reason about knowledge itself.

I am trained in both interpretive and quantitative traditions. I work comfortably with survey methods — scale development, factor analysis, statistical and structural equation modelling, and path analysis — and with mixed-method tools. That methodological breadth reflects a conviction that the social study of science education needs both the precision of measurement and the depth of interpretation.
