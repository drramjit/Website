The work in brief

Reading the village as a theoretical object

My scholarship sits at the meeting point of classical sociological theory and grounded fieldwork in the Gangetic plains. The questions are old — how does caste survive? why does land matter? — but the answers keep changing as markets, migration, and the state rewrite the terms of rural life.

Rather than treating the village as a quaint remainder of tradition, I treat it as a laboratory where the largest questions of social order are worked out in miniature. The result is a body of work that is empirically stubborn and theoretically ambitious in equal measure — an attempt to make the local legible without flattening it.

Read the longer biography →

Word in the margin Legible — able to be clearly read or understood. To make society "legible" is to render its complex patterns interpretable to an outside observer, often the central task of the social scientist.

Three currents

What I am working on now

Ongoing · 2024–

The Tenant's Ledger

A multi-village study of how land-leasing arrangements quietly reorganise caste hierarchy when ownership and cultivation come apart.

Ongoing · 2023–

Migration & the Left-Behind

How circular labour migration to cities reshapes authority, marriage, and gender within the households that remain in the village.

Archival · 2025–

Caste in the Census

A historical sociology of how colonial and post-colonial enumeration practices manufactured the very categories they claimed only to record.
