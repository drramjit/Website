The work in brief

Science as a social field, the classroom as its laboratory

My doctoral research at IIT Patna explores the knowledge base of the nature of science among Indian students — a sociological study grounded in Bihar. Rather than treating science as a neutral body of facts handed down to learners, I examine it as a social field: shaped by epistemology, pedagogy, and the wider public understanding of what science is and how it works.

The questions cut across the sociology of education and Science, Technology & Society (STS): How is scientific literacy actually assessed? What does it mean to think "scientifically"? And how do textbooks, classrooms, and society together construct a student's image of science? My aim is to make the social life of scientific knowledge legible — and, in doing so, to inform a more equitable science education.

Word in the margin Epistemology — the branch of philosophy concerned with the nature, sources, and limits of knowledge. To study the epistemology of science is to ask not what science knows, but how it knows it, and how those ways of knowing are taught.
